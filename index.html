Kayla Robertson

Developer, designer, journalist

I recently graduated with a degree in journalism from the University of Montana, where I found my passion for telling stories through design. What began with designing print newspapers and infographics quickly moved to a love of all things digital journalism -- interactive data vizualizations, maps, web development and accessibility.

I moved to Berlin, Germany after graduation and attended a JavaScript based full-stack web development course.

Previously, I worked at the Denver Post as the graphics intern. In college, I co-built and designed the website for the Meth Effect, managed infographics and page design at the Montana Kaimin and managed student web designers in building accessible online classes. I occasionally freelance.

I believe good design and accurate, communicative web graphics are crucial to the future of journalism and storytelling.
