Kayla Robertson

Developer, designer, journalist

I recently graduated with a degree in journalism from the University of Montana, where I found my passion for telling stories through design. What began with designing print newspapers and infographics quickly moved to a love of all things involving digital storytelling -- interactive data vizualizations, maps, web development and accessibility.

I moved to Berlin, Germany after graduation to attended a full-stack web development course, focused on JavaScript.

Previously, I worked at the Denver Post as the graphics intern. In college, I co-built and designed the website for the Meth Effect, managed infographics and page design at the Montana Kaimin and managed student web designers in building accessible online classes at the Innovation Studio. I also occasionally freelance.

I believe good design and accurate, communicative web graphics are crucial to the future of journalism and storytelling.
